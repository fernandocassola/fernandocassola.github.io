About

My academic and professional activity is driven by a simple question: how can digital systems become more meaningful, more collaborative, and more useful in real-world contexts? I work on the design of interactive platforms that connect data, services, users, and digital representations in ways that support decision-making, learning, and innovation.

Profile

Academic identity

I teach and supervise in areas related to web technologies, information systems, and service-oriented architectures, while also developing research on extended reality, digital twins, immersive dashboards, and interoperable platforms. My work often bridges conceptual frameworks and concrete applications, with a strong emphasis on usability, real deployment scenarios, and multidisciplinary collaboration.

Approach

Research with application value

I am particularly interested in solutions that move beyond prototypes and demonstrate value in practice. This includes systems for immersive training, scientific data visualization, human-centred navigation support, AI-enhanced assistance, and virtual choreographies for complex digital twin ecosystems.

Research

My research explores how immersive environments, interoperable architectures, and intelligent digital interfaces can support analysis, collaboration, and action in complex domains.

Research line

Digital twins and virtual choreographies

Development of domain-agnostic frameworks for coordinating and connecting digital twins through virtual choreographies, enabling interoperable and scalable interactions across distributed systems and heterogeneous platforms.

Research line

Extended reality for navigation, training and visualization

Design of XR-based applications for guidance, interaction, monitoring, and collaborative exploration, including augmented and virtual reality experiences tailored to different users, devices, and environments.

Research line

Scientific dashboards and immersive analytics

Creation and evaluation of dashboard platforms that facilitate understanding of complex scientific or operational data, including configurable interfaces, immersive environments, and support for multimodal interaction.

Selected Projects

These projects represent some of the themes that structure my recent work: interoperability, immersive systems, digital twins, scientific platforms, and applied innovation.

Featured project

VChor — Virtual Choreographies for Digital Twins

A framework for structuring and coordinating interactions between digital twins in distributed environments. This work addresses interoperability, orchestration, and abstraction challenges, and has been applied to complex scenarios such as oil spill simulation and ocean-related digital twin ecosystems.

Featured project

Immersive dashboards for offshore energy and ocean visualization

Development of multiplatform immersive dashboards targeting devices such as Meta Quest 3, Apple Vision Pro, smartphones, and AR/VR environments. These systems support the exploration of operational and environmental data through spatial, interactive and collaborative visual interfaces.

Featured project

XR platforms for guidance, assistance and training

Research and prototyping of configurable immersive systems that assist users through visual augmented reality, intelligent audio interaction, virtual guides, and behavioural analysis. These ideas support future work in indoor navigation, contextual assistance, and immersive training platforms.

Featured project

NASYNC — mobile support application for NAS interaction

A mobile application designed to simplify interaction with a NAS environment by enabling targeted synchronization of photo content between a storage system and a smartphone, with a focus on usability, practical integration, and interface quality.

Research and innovation

Immersive serious games and motion-based interaction

Design of interactive systems that promote engagement and physical activity through gesture-based interaction. This includes serious game experiences developed with Unity and Kinect for populations such as older adults.

Strategic coordination

Research leadership and European project involvement

Participation and coordination roles in international and European research initiatives, including projects connected to semantic interoperability, digital twins, applied AI, climate-related challenges, and defence-oriented innovation programmes.

Teaching

Teaching is a central part of my academic identity. I value classes that connect concepts to real problems, encourage reflection, and create opportunities for students to build, question, and apply knowledge.

Courses

Pedagogy

How I approach teaching

My classes often begin with informal prompts, reflection moments or challenges that connect academic content to real life.
